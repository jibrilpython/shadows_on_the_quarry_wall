# Shadows on the Quarry Wall

Description:

Shadows on the Quarry Wall is a mobile application for collectors of industrial stone-cutting antiquities, historians of masonry engineering, and keepers of architectural craftsmanship heritage, dedicated to cataloging the manual splitting wedges, hand-cranked stone boring drills, and mechanical templates that shaped the granite, marble, and limestone monuments of the world before the era of diamond-wire saws and computerized CNC routing tables.
Systematize your collection of the implements that carved out history—from hand-forged steel plug-and-feather wedge sets used to split massive boulders to brass-geared stone masons' leveling arcs, from heavy cast-iron core-drills to intricate zinc profile gauges used to replicate decorative gothic cathedral moldings.
Create a digital archive of heavy industry by recording the instrument type, manufacturer, temperature range, era, and the specific steel mill, foundry, or ceramic kiln each specimen once calibrated.

## Bespoke Object Fields (Model "Masonry Tool"):

•	Bedding Plane Index: A unique, system-generated cryptographic ledger code (e.g., SQW-STONE-1149-MARB-G).

•	Implementation Class: The specific category of extraction or shaping tool (e.g., Plug-and-Feather Set, Tracing Chisel, Core Boring Rig, Molding Template, Mason's Leveling Arc).

•	Artisan Hallmark: Fictionalized tool works and industrial stone foundries (e.g., Bedford Iron & Wedge Co., Keystone Masonry Tools, Aetheric Quarry Supply).

•	Dimensional Cleavage Capacity: The physical size limits or maximum splitting depth the tool handles (e.g., 18-inch Bore Depth, 4-inch Wedge Clearance, 1:1 Profile Match).

•	Cutting Edge Metallurgy: The exact structural composition of the active tool blade or wedge (e.g., Fire-tempered high-carbon steel, cold-chiseled wrought iron, reinforced tungsten-alloy tip).

•	Template Geometric Pattern: For profiling tools, the architectural style or curve geometry it was cut to replicate (e.g., Ogee arch profile, Roman Doric fluting, beveled chamfer scale).

•	Chamber Dimensions & Mass: The total length of the boring shaft or wedge assembly along with the dry mass in kilograms.

•	Structural Soundness: The current physical state of the metal or frame (e.g., Micro-fracture presence, edge dulling percentage, striking-head mushrooming level).

•	Excavation Ground Zero: The fictionalized historic quarry, cathedral build site, or railway tunnel project where the tool was actively deployed (e.g., Forgotten Vermont marble pit, historic trans-alpine rail tunnel, abandoned coastal limestone cliffworks).
